About the Crop:

Jute - JRO 524 is a high-yielding variety known for its strong fiber quality and adaptability. It thrives in well-drained, loamy to clayey soils with a pH of 5.5 to 7.0. The crop requires approximately 1,000-1,200 mm of water per acre, which can be managed through rainfall or irrigation. Typically, JRO 524 yields around 2,500-3,000 kg of raw jute per acre. Profit margins are generally favorable, ranging from 15-25%, depending on market prices and production costs.

  • Soil Composition: well-drained loamy soils with a pH range of 5.5 to 7.0
  • Water need per Acre: 3.28 to 3.94feet
  • Potential Dangers: Soil Degradation, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Diammonium Phosphate, Organic Matter, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Emamectin benzoate ,Thiamethoxam, Acetamiprid
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹7,500 to ₹15,000
